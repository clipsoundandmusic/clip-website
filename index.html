A Song For Elephants

Art Installation

“Step into this auditory experience and submerge yourself in the feeling of sound. In collaboration with Colchester Zoo, CLIP have stretched, twisted and pulled various the vocalisations of the smallest animals into a frequency range which elephants use to communicate - sub 20hz.”

In collaboration with Colchester zoo, we recorded a selection of their most vocal residents and use these vocalisations for an installation within the gallery, as part of the FLIPSIDE exibition. We were interested in the translation of sound, how could the smallest critter communicate with one of the largest mammals? We wanted to build a musical google translate. So, we began by pitching down the chirps of the dart frog by a few octaves, we ‘translated’ it into the frequency range that elephants use for communication, sub bass.

  • Date: October 2016
  • Category: Installation

Wall of Sounds

Art Installation

Using a group of transducer speakers, we converted the gallery walls into a giant speaker, playing a collection of animal and human duets hidden within various surfaces - inviting visitors to explore the walls and discover the sounds hidden within. Using recordings from Colchester Zoo, Clip members were invited to create duets using an instrument of their choice

The installation will form part of Flipside exhibition, a two week audio visual festival which aims to transform your perspective of contemporary art and curated by YAK, Firstsite’s resident young art community.

  • Date: October 2016
  • Category: Installation

Sound Factory

Workshop

During the Andy Warhol exhibition at Firstsite, within the 'Digital Factory' residency space we created two very unique sonic spaces. We filled the smaller room with acoustic instruments and encouraged visitors to explore all the sounds they could make, such as putting a bell inside the type writer or bowing the glockenspiel. Inside this room we set up a handful of microphones and routed these into the second space, processing them through various pedals and audio toys.

This disconnection between the two spaces was really interesting. A twee nursery rhyme sang into the microphone suddenly sounded like a dystopian cinematic soundtrack with an ominous drone rumbling underneath. Although the glass wall between the two spaces allowed for some visual communication we found people were more interested in acting individually or rather collaborating with those in the same space rather than with those outside. The architecture of the spaces could definitely lead to more experiments in sonic communication, exploring ways of creating a dialogue between two sets of performers.

  • Date: April 2016
  • Category: Workshop

Graphic Scores

Workshop

Un-trained musicians can find musical notation "old fashioned" or even "un cool", without realising how versitile it can be. With these workshops we explore complex ideas like extended-playing techniques, emotion, dynamics, timbre - but instead of Italian terminology its with coloured pencils and shapes.

Once the participants had made their drinking straw kazoo, lollipop harmonica or paper cup drum they were then invited to explore all the sounds their creation could create. When they knew what sounds it could make, we then asked them to find a way of writing these down. This is where the session was its most ambitious, how can you draw a sound? Traditional notation is very confusing at first, and actually very poor at communicating emotion. So why not design your own system, this is what I was asking the children to do.

As shown in the pictures above, this could be coloured shapes for notes and relative size for volume, curved lines for pitch and shapes for extended technique. The only limit is your imagination! The children not only made their own instrument, but they wrote songs and created their own notation from scratch. Needless to say it was an incredibly rewarding session.

  • Date: March 2016
  • Category: Installation

Flow Collaboration

Performance

During FLIPSIDE festival, CLIP were invited to collaborate with FLOW - a dance group who experiment with 'flow toys' such as staffs, poi, hoops, juggling balls and much more. Both sets of performances, dancers and musicians, would be improvising around set moves and melodies and since we wouldnt be able to communicate at all during the performance, we worked together on a very basic graphic score.

This meant as musicians we would be responding to the dancers movements, whilst they performed within a pre-determinded structure. Throughout our weekly meetups we had been experimenting with looping, building up different sections of audio to improvise over the top of. Working within this framework we devised three 10 minute sections, with a bell curve for our dynamics and the straight dotted line denoting the frequency range.

With the dancers beginning on the floor, we played low bassy rumbles (with heavy use of pitch shifting guitar pedals) and then finished with the dancers high in the air and us playing the highester notes possible on our instruments.

  • Date: October 2016
  • Category: Performance

In (The) C

Performance

In our weekly meetups we often discuss the 'fear of changing key' when improvising as a group, especially with young and/or shy musicians. So, Terry Riley's 'In C' is a perfect piece to see how far we can push just one key.

In C is a response to the abstract academic serialist techniques devised by Schoenberg that dominated Western university composers for many decades and is often cited as the first minimalist composition. Continuing our theme of introducing experimental and avant garde ideas to young musicians, we're in the process of learning this piece but with a twist - that twist being the performance venue, the venue being the actual sea.

With support from the Young Classical Artists Trust - CLIP are working on the logistics of this performance to take a performance somewhere along the Essex coastline and engage a whole new audience into the wonderful world of experimental music, whilst also introducing a group of young musicians to classical music notation and creating oportunities for them to perform in new and exciting projects.
